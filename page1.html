Major Project: NFSA Web App

The web app prototype leverages the National Film and Sound Archive's API to provide an engaging and informative way to explore lobby cards, 11x14-inch movie promotional posters known for their rich visual communication. Targeting graphic designers and history enthusiasts, the app focuses on user needs such as exploring color usage and historical themes.

Key features include:

  1. Image-Based Library: Users can click images to reveal relevant information, sortable by color and publish date.
  2. Streamlined Design: Navigation is interest-driven, avoiding irrelevant elements like tabs or excessive text, ensuring a smooth and focused user experience.
  3. Unique Features: A feature enabling users to discover a randomly selected lobby card, adding an element of surprise and inspiration to their exploration.

When the development of this web app was completed, an indepth evaluation of experience a user may go through when utilising this web app was conducted.

  1. It first introduced and examined the potential users for this web app, identifying their intents and needs.
  2. Then, trials were developed and conducted based on user needs. The user testing phase was conducted by task prompts and post-trial interviews.
  3. Lastly, the evaluation took the results from the testing phases and discussed critical issues, minor issues, and positive observations, further identifying areas for improvement.
