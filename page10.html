Carwrap Design: BMW Competition Entry

The final design is a powerful and colourful design. It utilises popping colours that match Australia’s landscape and emerges white highlights that are sleek and legible. Not only does all the colours represent Australia’s landscape beauty, they represent key things. The bright red and orange colours represent Australia’s rich resources in its land, the soft greens represent green solutions and sustainable futures, and the blues represent nuclear power in relation to boiling water for energy.

Its abstract layout, including asymmetrical and symmetrical features, extends across the whole car, showcasing large themes that are versatile, just like nuclear power, an incredibly versatile tool for shaping the future. Numerous text displays are everywhere on this car, though not smothering it. The text effortlessly reads words and phrases that attribute nuclear power positively, encouraging those who read it to feel convicted or inspired. White texts, as well as patterns, have been chosen to represent purity, cleanliness, and beginnings.

The detail and thought going into this design has been well tested and well thought through. From the specific shade of colours chosen, and the words and phrases to communicate a message, all the way to the shapes, logos, and typography developed, and the maintenance of a consistent, seemless, and enveloping design.
